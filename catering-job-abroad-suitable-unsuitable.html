Catering job abroad: Suitable or unsuitable

Are you suitable or unsuitable for a hospitality

The hospitality industry is not an easy industry. Working hours vary enormously. Sometimes you work a shift from 12.00 noon to 01.00 at night. And the other time you're already ready at 22.00 and you can go home earlier.

You walk back and forth a lot with heavy trays and you always have to be friendly with the customer. Customers naturally want to be served by a cheerful person because they expect good service. Customers in the hospitality industry are usually always cheerful. That makes the work very diverse.

Everyone wants to have a chat with you, they give compliments about the work you do and of course that tip! There are both advantages and disadvantages to this industry. Read on to see if it is something for you and what you can earn from it!

Why work in the hospitality industry?

Clients in the hospitality industry are usually always cheerful. They go out for a nice meal and want to experience a pleasant evening. Because of this, they are often cheerful to the restaurant staff, so to you! That also makes your work more fun.

A part-time job in the hospitality industry can earn you extra money quickly and at short notice. If you need money in the short term for a holiday or a car you want to buy, the hospitality industry is an ideal option to quickly earn money.

It is also possible that you don't go on holiday for once. A job in the hospitality industry can then be a great holiday job! The customers you meet are often tourists. They have nice stories about what they have seen or maybe they just ask for things. Maybe you get an extra tip for this.

Personnel in the hospitality industry

In the hospitality industry you can expand your network unnoticed! You have Dutch customers, but businessmen who come for a bite to eat. Do you have a quick chat, or does a businessman leave his business card behind? Chances are he is interested in you!

What are the disadvantages of the hospitality industry?

Times vary a lot. That's why it's hard to plan and make time for yourself. It can also be debilitating on your body. Even if you've had a late night shift, when the next shift starts at noon on your schedule, you just have to rotate it.

Wages in the hospitality industry are not the highest and career opportunities are scarce.

Employment is very seasonal. In the summer there is a lot of demand for labour in the hospitality industry. However, when winter sets in, demand decreases sharply. If this is your permanent job, it can have a big impact on your fixed income.

Hospitality industry abroad?

Would you rather work abroad in the hospitality industry? That is also possible! Dutch tourists go every year to countries like Italy, France or Spain. There is always a demand for Dutch personnel abroad.
